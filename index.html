Space Man

Sci‑fi stealth survival action RPG about a stranded astronaut, an AI‑controlled station, and a drone companion.

A focused indie project seeking development funding to build a playable vertical slice: underground labyrinth exploration, AI surveillance, drone utility, survival crafting, anomaly encounters, and ship repair progression.

Project concept

Escape the station before it rewrites the maze.

The player descends into a hostile industrial labyrinth taken over by AI. Cameras, guards and organic anomalies control the route. Discovery can trigger a time‑limited trap sequence where the station rearranges itself and forces the player to run for an exit.

Target visual direction: stranded astronaut, buried facility, harsh daylight, abandoned machinery.

Core pitch

A compact sci‑fi survival game where stealth exploration, resource pressure and drone tools create short high‑tension expeditions into a maze‑like AI station.

Player objective

Find computer nodes, disable AI control systems, collect ship parts and return to the crash site to repair the vessel and escape the planet.

Production position

The project is currently in early development. Funding is required to produce a convincing 10–15 minute playable vertical slice.

Gameplay loop

Explore. Survive. Extract. Repair.

The design is built around repeatable arcade‑RPG expeditions rather than a huge open world. The ship acts as the home base; labyrinth runs deliver resources, data and repair parts.

Enter the labyrinthTeleport or descend into the last known sector.
Avoid AI surveillanceCameras and AI guards can detect the player and trigger emergency states.
Use the droneLight dark corridors, scan anomalies, find paths and support defensive actions.
Collect data and materialsGather oxygen, water components, medical resources, boards, transistors and ship parts.
Escape or fightAnomaly contact can shift into a turn‑based RPG encounter with drone support.
Return to the shipCraft supplies, install parts, prepare for the next descent.
Interior target: readable traversal spaces, hard-surface corridors, sand intrusion and atmospheric lighting.

Interface direction

Functional sci‑fi HUD, not decoration.

The UI direction is a black high-contrast sci-fi interface with thin white technical frames and magenta accents for active selection and danger states.

Main menu direction: clean black HUD, white frame language, magenta active state.
Inventory direction: equipment, data, consumables, health, stamina and oxygen systems.
